Amnis Finance Overview

Amnis Finance is a leading liquid staking derivatives (LSD) protocol within the Aptos ecosystem, designed to help users maximize staking rewards and unlock liquidity through its innovative dual-token model (amAPT and stAPT). Below is a summary of its core features, ecosystem integrations, and additional information:

Core Mechanisms and Revenue Model
Simple Three-Step Staking Process

Stake: Connect your wallet and stake APT to receive an equivalent amount of amAPT (a stablecoin pegged 1:1 to APT).

Earn: Stake amAPT to generate stAPT, which accrues real-time staking rewards at an APR of 8.5% (actual rates may vary; currently up to 8.56%).

Use in DeFi: Deploy stAPT or amAPT across Aptos ecosystem DeFi protocols to stack additional rewards through lending, liquidity provision, and more.

Dual-Token Design

amAPT: Directly represents staked APT and can be used for instant redemptions or DeFi activities (e.g., liquidity pools).

stAPT: An auto-compounding token that accumulates staking rewards over time (similar to an interest-bearing asset). Its exchange ratio increases as rewards accumulate.

Additional Information and Core Advantages
Market Size and Growth

Leading TVL: As of the end of 2024, Amnis manages a Total Value Locked (TVL) of $390 million, accounting for 30% of Aptos ecosystem TVL, with over 360,000 users.

Competitive APR: A base APR of 8.56% is the highest in the Aptos ecosystem, outperforming some LSD protocols on Ethereum and Solana.

Technical Highlights

Dynamic Reward Distribution: Staking rewards are automatically distributed every 2 hours, optimizing compounding efficiency.

Validator Node Optimization: Staked assets are automatically allocated to the best-performing nodes via an on-chain scoring system to maximize returns.

Anti-Arbitrage Mechanism: Features like small fees and delayed reward distribution prevent short-term arbitrage.

Future Plans and Governance

AMI Governance Token: The AMI token will launch in Q1 2025, allowing holders to participate in protocol governance (e.g., parameter adjustments, revenue allocation) and potentially receive airdrops through Retroactive activities.

Decentralized Governance: A gradual transition to a DAO model will enable the community to decide on protocol upgrades and fund strategies.

Security and Partnerships

Audits and Risk Control: Audited by OtterSec and MoveBit, with price stabilization mechanisms (e.g., market buybacks of amAPT) in place.

Capital Support: Backed by $2 million in funding from institutions like Borderless Capital and OKX Ventures, with collaborations across 50+ ecosystem projects.

Conclusion
Amnis Finance simplifies staking processes, offers high-yield strategies, and integrates extensively with DeFi, making it a core infrastructure within the Aptos ecosystem. Users can flexibly manage assets through its dual-token model and amplify returns using leverage, liquidity mining, and other tools. With the upcoming launch of the AMI token, its community governance and decentralization efforts are worth watching.
